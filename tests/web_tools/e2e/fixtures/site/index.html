Pythagorean theorem replay page

The theorem can be written as an equation relating the lengths of the sides a, b and the hypotenuse c.

The formula is sometimes called the Pythagorean equation.

It states that the area of the square whose side is the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the areas of the squares on the other two sides.

The Plimpton 322 tablet records Pythagorean triples from Babylonian times.[1]

The theorem is also known as the Gougu theorem.

  • The area of a triangle is half the area of any parallelogram.

And likewise, at all moments in time, the area is always a 2 + b 2.

The Arithmetical Classic of the Gnomon and the Circular Paths of Heaven is referenced in historical summaries.

Pythagoras lived c. 570 – c. 495 BC.

The familiar algebraic statement is a² + b² = c².
